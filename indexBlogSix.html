Top 7 Reasons to Visit Nepal

January 29, 2021 / by Troopers

Are you planning to travel to Nepal. If so, here are the top seven reasons to visit Nepal:


  1. Art and architecture

    Speckled with an astonishing array of ancient temples and palaces and numerous exemplary works of art and architecture, Nepal stands as a testimony to aesthetics. There are several monuments enlisted in the UNESCO world heritage sites as well as museums displaying ancient artwork. Your trip to the Kathmandu valley will be blessed by intricate woodcarvings and stone sculpture dotted in the backstreets and medieval architects at every turn of Durbar Squares.


  2. Adventurous country

    Why not get out of the city pleasures and give a shot to the thrilling adventures? Why not get the wildest experiences in the concrete jungles and soar above the highest peaks of the world, flying like a bird? There is an immense pleasure in biking in bumpy rough trails and endeavour the aerial life. If you love canyoning in the majestic waterfalls and climb the slippery rocks, challenging yourself physically and mentally, then Nepal is a perfect place for you to land on!


  3. Nepalese food

    As a multi-ethnic and multi-cultural country, each community has a definite food culture here. Although, the regular Nepali meal is daal, bhat and tarkari accompanied by pickle. Kathmandu is widely renowned as the food capital of the world as you can’t find international cuisine as of Kathmandu in any other places of the world. You can eat like a king here with menus displaying the names of all continental and local foods. Or you can enjoy the local taste during homestays and trekkings.

    4. ...

  4. Naturally gifted country

    With each step you take ahead, you can experience the immense beauty and creation of nature. As for the mountains and peaks, Nepal is a home for the highest peak of the world along with other 8 peaks that falls under the topmost 10 highest peaks of the world. If we talk about lakes and rives, we have Tilocho lake here, which is renowned for its location in the highest altitude, along with other beautiful lakes, rivers and rivulets. Further, the breathtaking view of soaring waterfalls.


  5. The highest peak

    Nepal is renowned as the land of Himalayas as it shelters the 8 out of 14 above 8,000m sky- scraping mountain peaks. Further, a journey along the great Himalayan Trails will introduce you to the famous hallmarks including deep gorges, high mountain passes, soaring waterfalls and sapphire mountain lakes. Either trek to the spectacular mountain panoramas or fly over the highest peaks rejoicing the gorgeous view of snow and wonderful sceneries.


  6. Culture and festivals

    Due to diverse cultures and religions, every day is a day of celebration of amazing festivals of varying rituals. Though, diversity and harmony co-exist here with respect of each other’s core cultural values. The everyday schedule of Nepalese people is intimately related to festivals.


  7. Hassle-free visa and economic

    Nepal tries its best to make things easy for travellers. Tourist visas of 15/30/90 days are available on arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport at any currency and on USD on other land borders open to foreigners. If you are a South Asian (except Indian) or Chinese and you are entering only once in a calendar year, you will enjoy free visa here.

Homestay in Nepal

January 29, 2021 / by Troopers

‘Atithi Debo Bhawa.’

The popular Sanskrit saying translates to ‘Guests are the form of god’. Nepalese people feels honored to have guests in their home and treat them like a family member. That’s how the concept of homestay developed.

Homestay is just a home converted to a hotel. Guests are either given a separate quarter valuing their privacy or let them be with other family members engaging in local traditions and way of life.

Why homestay?


  1. See Things in a Local Way

    There are innumerable wonderful things about local cultures which you would never find in search engines and even your guide may not have in-depth knowledge on local way of life. You can dance with the locals, learn the local folk, dress in a local way or learn pottery and enjoy the local games learning new words. Share with them your cultures and get a return gift of overwhelming hospitality and surprisingly creative skills.


  2. Get delighted With Local Cuisine

    Explore the amazing taste of home cooked food and learn local recipe to make your friends surprised of your magical skills after you return your homeland. The taste and recipe may vary accordingly with climate and culture of people which varies to a great extent in every 1km you travel. So, ready for the secret recipe and divine flavor of Nepalese food?


  3. Personalized Services

    The best part of homestay is you will be treated as guest and not as a client. So, you can freely share how you feel and they will feel delighted to fulfill your wishes. You can even get engaged with them in their routine activities and ask what you want to taste or learn. You’ll be given proper care if you are tired or sick. And oh, there’s a magical healing power in local herbs. Don’t forget to try such wonders of Himalayas!

    4. ...

  4. Make New Friends

    You never know who you will encounter the next. Maybe a renowned musician, a pottery expert or a professional. But let them be whoever, homestay owners are often willing to know a lot about you and your country. So, most of the times, they are a chatty folk. And moreover, if there are children. So, don’t miss the wonderful opportunity of gaining new experiences and unique traditions with them. Further, you won’t feel loner in a new place with them. It would feel like one of their dearest relative visiting them after a long time.


  5. Get Closer to Culturally Rich Experiences

    Almost every day is celebrated like a special occasion in Nepal with different cultural significance backed up with legendary stories and achievements. So, get ready to explore the filthy cultures and learn some new skills. Visit the famous cultural sites and get to know the amazing history it reflects. Celebrate every festival in a local way and take fantastic memories home.


  6. Feel at Home

    A homestay is after all, someone’s home and there’s always an emotional attachment with the word ‘home’. So, you will feel at home with proper care and respect. Get the most out of your vacation when you are here without worrying about anything.

Your Health Guide While Visiting Nepal

January 29, 2021 / by Troopers

‘Large hospitals and clinics in Nepal are centralized to Kathmandu valley and big cities only. Though there are small health posts providing basic treatment and medicines with normal checkup, you can’t expect the availability of necessary medicines and proper checkup everywhere you visit. You might definitely go on trekking up the Himalayas through steep pathway with no trace of residential area till your eyes could see. In such areas, prospects of hospital remain far beyond. So, you are suggested to carry basic first aid kit and your medicine (if any) not found elsewhere everywhere you travel. And don’t forget to read the health risk outlined in your itinerary or as description provided by your travel guide.

In case you catch severe illness or there are some serious medical conditions, you will be evacuated to Kathmandu or overseas (to your home country) immediately.

So, before you venture into the land of exotic landscapes with skyscraping mountains, deep gorges, charismatic people, diverse culture and unique traditions, prepare yourself well so that you won’t miss even a moment to delve deep into the gorgeous lush forest with rare flora and fauna. Here are some useful tips to care your health while traveling Nepal:


  • Purchase a travel insurance policy that covers all health risks.

  • Consult your physician regarding immunization against any tropical disease before visiting.

  • Always carry water bottle or energy drinks so as to remain hydrated in higher altitude.

  • Check the altitude of every place before visiting so that you can adopt preventives for altitude sickness.

...

  • Make sure you are drinking pure water and fresh foods. Remember tap water in Nepal is not fit for drinking.

  • If you are going on trekking or mountaineering, get some physical fitness training before walking.

  • Do not swim in unknown lakes or rivers as there might be threat of poisonous animals.

  • If you are planning for adventurous activities, do it under the supervision of your guide reading the safety guidelines.

  • You may get leeches and other insects in most of the Nepalese forests, especially in wetter areas. Don’t panic as they won’t do any harm.

  • If you feel unwell on climbing higher, stop and if the condition gets severe, don’t force yourself walk uphill and descend and rest until you feel better.


Better if You Carry

  • Water purifiers

  • Cold creams and sunscreen creams

  • Bandages and other wound dressings

  • Paracetamol or other pain relievers

  • Your regular medicines for headache and fever

  • Anti-inflammatory creams for joint ache and muscle pain

  • Antiseptic in case you get cuts

  • Motion sickness tablets and thermometer

  • Toilet paper and tissues

Social Etiquettes in Nepal

January 29, 2021 / by Troopers

As Nepal is a popular tourist destination inviting millions of tourists every year, people here are familiar to the cultures of other countries too. So, they won’t make it a big issue whatever you do. But it is better if you respect their cultures and value the precious time-bound traditions.

Whether you are visiting religious places or casually talking to locals, it is a good idea to keep in mind a few etiquette pointers as mentioned below:


  1. Do not Shake Hands

    Physical touch is reserved only for close friends and relatives in Nepal. So, don’t reach out to shake hands or giving a hug to unknown people. Be more conscious if the other member is of opposite sex.

    You can simply greet ‘Namaste’ joining both hands to anyone anytime. You can learn local languages or gestures of greeting as the culture and religion of people keeps on changing every km you travel. However, you can greet ‘Namaste’ to anyone here.


  2. Always Finish Your Food

    You can ask your host if you are allergic to some foods or you can’t take it due to medical conditions. But it is not a good idea to waste food after taking a portion of it. You can just take a little first to taste and ask them for more if you like it.

    Make sure you don’t walk over anyone, food or paper as it is considered disrespectful. Paper is worshipped as a form of Goddess Saraswoti and even if the food wrapper is of paper, throw it in a dustbin and be careful not to cross it over. Touching someone with your feet and sharing things with left hand is considered inauspicious. So, always use right hand to eat and take or give things.

    Don’t take a sip of someone else’s bottle touching with your lips or offer the food you have already bitten as it is considered rude. Sharing same plate is not okay unless you are close to each other.

    Further, don’t use your own used spoon to serve food as the food once touched is not acceptable in Nepal unless you are in close circle or are relatives.


    3. ...

  3. Etiquettes for Cultural/Religious Site Visit

    In some of the Hindu temples, non-Hindus are not allowed to enter. Leather utensils are not allowed within temple premises and women during their periods are restricted to worship God. Before worshipping, most Nepalese walk around the temple in clockwise direction. Encircling the temple in anti-clockwise direction is considered inauspicious.

    Take off your shoes before entering the temple premises and be careful not to burn out the burning candle. Always offer flowers to God either with right or both hands. The best way of sharing things is using right hand and touching the elbow of right hand with left hand.

    Remember that some temples and cultural sites restrict photography inside the premises. Have a look for the board suggesting restriction so as to avoid fines.

    Though the western culture is influencing Nepal at a rapid pace, revealing outfits are not still considered good. So, dress appropriately while you are visiting especially the rural parts of the country.


  4. Be Careful About Your Outfit

    Though the western culture is influencing Nepal at a rapid pace, revealing outfits are not still considered good. So, dress appropriately while you are visiting especially the rural parts of the country.


  5. Do Not Whistle.

    It is somehow okay to whistle outside (though not appreciated) but don’t even think about whistling inside someone’s house. It is believed that whistling inside would invite bad luck and considered rude if you whistle at someone.

    Likewise, be careful how you node to ‘yes’ or ‘no’. A sideways tilt of head conveys ‘yes’ or you agree to something, not ‘no’ as in western culture. You can simply raise your palm and wave as if you are fixing your bracelet to say ‘no’.

Safety of Tourists in Nepal

January 29, 2021 / by Troopers

Are you wondering whether to buy a ticket or cancel the flight to Nepal because of the rumors you heard about? Of course Nepal is one of the most fascinating places to visit once in a lifetime. However, it can’t be said that you won’t come along any shortcomings during your trip to Nepal. Every country has some unresolved issues, whether it be great or small. Nepal is no exception to these.

The land of Himalayas with the highest peak of the world, Mt. Everest and other 8 mountains above the height of 8,000m, the birthplace of Lord Buddha, the country of pristine waterfalls and grassy meadows, one of the few countries that never got colonized, deep gorges, vivid and harmonized cultures and religions, the country of temples with 10 UNESCO listed world heritage sites, charismatic people, unlimited fun on adventurous activities and heart-warming hospitality- with no shortage of things to travel and explore even within a year, Nepal is an ideal destination for travelers. However, everyone is concerned for their safety and give a second thought thinking about the problems they might face during travel. So, let’s get you clear about all the rumors that has been an unnecessary fuss and creating fear in the mind of enthusiastic travelers.

Take care of below mentioned things so you will enjoy your trip in peace and unlimited happiness. These are not major issues but you would better know these things before planning your trip to Nepal.


  1. Social Etiquettes in Nepal

    You must be well informed about the diverse cultures and religions of Nepal. Yet all Nepalese live in harmony, you would better be informed about the cultural groups residing in area you are travelling. Learn few words including local greeting styles apart from ‘Namaste’. It will make you feel loved and honored. Be informed about social etiquettes so as to not offend the locals. Read the etiquette rules to be followed in Nepal HERE


  2. Adventurous Activities

    Nepal is one of the top destinations for adventurous tourism. If you are thinking about hiking or trekking on your own, make sure you make necessary arrangements of your stay and safety equipment beforehand. We advise you to go on a group or at least with a friend. You may also hire a guide as the trekking trails might be confusing and you can easily lose tracks on the thick forest. See the altitude of place you are going on so as not to be the victim of altitude sickness or frostbite.

    You might want to challenge yourself rafting in the whitewater of Bhote Koshi or Trishuli, rock climbing, mountaineering, paragliding above the mesmerizing Phewa Lake and go on the bone-chilling mountain trails for biking. You may want to go on the second highest Bungee Jumping of the world above Bhote Koshi River or climb the mighty mountains. But whatever you do, we recommend you to get informed about the possible threats first and do on the guidance of your guide only.


  3. Pollution

    The first thing you will notice on getting out of the airport might be the dusty air and crowd of people. Once you get out of the airport, you can see the valley is so polluted than the wonderful stories you have heard about cultural heritages and scenic landscapes. So, please bring high quality mask and medicines if you are allergic to dust.

    4. ...

  4. Water Safety

    Direct water from the tap is not safe for drinking here, not even the water from your hotel tap. So, use boiled or filtered water. You can also use water purifiers if you like but we advise you not to drink water directly from the tap. Ice might also have been made from tap water and fruits might have been washed directly from there. So, please be conscious on water safety if you don’t want to spend your vacation dreaming about all the fascinating things you could have done if you were not in hospital bed.


  5. Network Availability

    Most of the Nepalese villages are still out of reach of electricity. Even in big cities like Kathmandu, you cannot expect reliable undisruptive internet all the time.


  6. Food Safety

    Make sure you inform your travel partner if you are allergic to some foods or have some food restriction due to medical conditions. Most of the Nepalese foods are often spicy. Though you can order whatever you want whether it be severely hot, mild or no spicy at all in restaurants, you may not get that chance in some traditional servings.


  7. Irregular Flights

    If you are thinking of taking a flight, remember that Nepalese flights depend heavily on the frequently changing climatic conditions. So, don’t risk your important works depending heavily on the timely flight. Make sure you have some extra essentials in your backpack every time so as to be safe on emergency.


  8. Street Dogs and Monkeys

    You will encounter with dogs in every corner of Nepal. But they won’t do you any harm unless you tease or hurt them. To be safe from monkeys especially around temples, don’t carry anything fancy or foods in your hands as they might snatch it away from you.


  9. Road Traffic

    There are no traffic lights in most of the Nepalese roads and if there are, they are not followed properly. So, be careful while crossing the busy highways of Nepal. Take overhead bridge or wait for the signal of traffic police before crossing.


    10. Politics in Nepal is being stable than it has been in years. There are no threats of political issues and crimes here. However, the chances of obstruction due to landslide, flood and other natural disasters can’t be taken lightly. Take your travel insurance policy with coverage of thefts and prolonged stay. It’s better to prevent such incidents so as not to ruin your beautiful vacation in the lap of Himalayas!
