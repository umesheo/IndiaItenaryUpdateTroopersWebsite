Annapurna Panorama Trek

About The Place

Annapurna Panorama Trek is an incredible trekking adventure in the Annapurna foothills. This adventure is an extended version of Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek. You will be able to explore more of nature, mountains and the culture in the region while you are on this adventure.

Itinerary

  • Day 1 - Arrival at Airport

    Hotel
    • Arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport
    • Transfer to hotel
    • Meal arrangement: Dinner

  • Day 2 - Kathmandu Valley Sightseeing

    Hotel
    • After breakfast, visit several of the UNESCO World Heritage Sites, including Kathmandu’s Durbar Square with its old royal palace and nearly 60 ancient temples. From here, travel to the top of Swayambhu (Monkey Temple) to experience an awesome view of the Kathmandu Valley
    • Swayambhu is the oldest Buddhist sacred site in the Kathmandu Valley. From here, travel to the ancient city of Patan to view its Durbar Square and ancient temples
    • The final stop is Pashupatinath, the most sacred Hindu temple complex in Nepal and a pilgrimage site for Hindus from around the world
    • Meal arrangement: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
    • Overnight stay at Hotel

  • Day 3: Kathmandu to Pokhara (1400m)

    5-6 hours Hotel 200.9 km
    • We leave the capital city of Nepal after breakfast in the early morning, taking a first class tourist bus to the lakeside city of Pokhara
    • It’s about a six-hour journey, and it passes through the Trisuli River valley before crossing this world-class rafting river and heading westward to Pokhara
    • Arrive in Pokhara at your hotel and enjoy dining out at Lakeside with its many restaurants and fun nightlife
    • Meal arrangement: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
    • Overnight stay at hotel

  • Day 4: Travel to Birethani, trek to Tikheldunga (1577m)

    1hr 30min 4-5 hours 6-7 hours Hotel 51 km
    • We rise and take a bus/van to Nayapul, the starting point of our trek into the Annapurnas. We begin trekking at Nayapul, rising in elevation as we pass through a variety of vegetation from sub-tropical to almost alpine
    • We arrive at the village of Tikhedunga to spend the night at a tea house. Great views of the mountains along the way
    • Meal arrangement: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
    • Overnight stay at hotel

  • Day 5: Tikhedhunga - Ghorepani (2,874m)

    6-7 hours Hotel 12.9 km
    • Our destination is the village of Ghorepani, famous for its waters on the main salt road to Tibet. Today’s trail climbs the stone steps that the local village development committees have installed to aid in travel
    • These steps were built for donkeys and humans as these are the highways of the Himalayas
    • There are lush forests and crystal clear streams along the way as we pass through beautiful villages with terraced fields. We will spend the night in Ghorepani
    • Meal arrangement: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
    • Overnight stay at hotel

  • Day 6: Ghorepani - Poonhill (hike for Sunrise) - Tadapani (2,706m)

    6-7 hours Hotel 13.6 km
    • We will rise well before sunrise to make our way to the top of Poon Hill (elev. 3,210 m/10,531 ft., 2.9km) to witness the Himalayas’ most famous sunrise. We stand in the early morning as the first rays of the effulgent sun begin to shoot above the mountains
    • The peaks are grey at first, then they begin to turn reddish orange as the sun continues to rise. Finally, the snowcapped peaks turn a brilliant white as the sun rises above the mountains. From here, we can see a number of high peaks, including Manaslu and Dhaulagiri
    • From Poon Hill, we return for breakfast in Ghorepani, and then make our way to the village of Tadapani (2,706m,10.7km). It’s about five hours of trekking to reach our tea house for the night
    • Meal arrangement: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
    • Overnight stay at hotel

  • Day 7: Tadapani - Ghandruk (1,940m)

    3-4 hours Tea House 6.5 km
    • Today we are descending, but along the way we will have many opportunities to see the snowcapped peaks of the Annapurnas. We will pass through the village of Chame to reach the Gurung village of Ghandruk
    • The Gurungs are the famous soldiers of the Gurkhas in the British and Indian armies. Known as fierce fighters, you wouldn’t know it when you meet them in their villages
    • They are also known for exquisite hospitality with big smiles and open arms. Enjoy the night at a tea house in Ghandruk
    • Meal arrangement: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
    • Overnight stay at Hotel

  • Day 8: Ghandruk - Birethani(1100m) -Drive to Pokhara

    Hotel
    • It’s the final day of trekking as we continue downhill to reach the village of Birethanti
    • Here we pick up local transportation back to the city of Pokhara where we will spend the night at a nice hotel where you can get a hot shower.
    • Meal arrangement: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
    • Overnight stay at Hotel

  • Day 9: Sightseeing in Pokhara

    Hotel
    • For many people, Pokhara is the highlight of a trip into the Himalayas. Located on the eastern shore of Phewa Lake, this city is nestled at the edge of the Annapurnas. From here, the mountains overshadow the city. We will travel in the morning to see the sunrise from Sarangkot Hill
    • We will take in Devi Falls and then go across the street and into the earth to visit the cave temple. There will be an opportunity to go boating on Phewa Lake and visit the sacred temple on the only island within the lake
    • See the Seti Gorge, walk into Mahendra Cave, visit sacred temples and much more as you see the sights in Pokhara. Return to your hotel for the evening
    • Meal arrangement: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
    • Overnight stay at Hotel

  • Day 10: Return to Kathmandu (1400m)

    5-6 hours Hotel 200.7 km
    • Say goodbye to Pokhara as your first class luxury bus takes you back to your hotel in Kathmandu
    • Meal arrangement: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
    • Overnight stay at Hotel

  • Day 11: Sightseeing in Kathmandu

    Hotel
    • There is so much more to see in the Kathmandu Valley, and today, we will journey to the ancient city of Bhatkapur. This was once its own kingdom and today the entire city has been declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site
    • There will be a visit to Boudanath, the largest Buddhist Stupa in Asia which houses the largest Tibetan refugee settlement in Nepal. Spend the night at your hotel
    • Meal arrangement: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
    • Overnight stay at Hotel

  • Day 12: Departure from Kathmandu

    • After checking out of your hotel, travel to the airport to catch your flight to your next destination. Before leaving, be sure to discuss other trips in Nepal and the surrounding countries with your friends at Buddha Treks & Expedition
    • Meal arrangement: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
    • Overnight stay at Hotel
