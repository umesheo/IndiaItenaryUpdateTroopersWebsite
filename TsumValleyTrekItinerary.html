Tsum Valley Trek

About The Place

Tsum Valley is believed to be one of Buddhismâ€™s sacred hidden valleys, where spiritual progress is facilitated, and where you will find old monasteries and thriving communities of monks and nuns. For centuries, people in the valley have depended on subsistence agriculture, grazing herds of yak, and trading with Tibet.

Itinerary

  • Day 1 : Arrival at Kathmandu (1,400m)

    Hotel
    • Arrival at Kathmandu (Tribhuvan International Airport)
    • Short brief about trek to Clients
    • Dinner & Overnight Stay at Hotel

  • Day 2 : Kathmandu - Soti Khola

    9-10 hours Lodge 136 km
    • A 9-10 hour's of drive from Kathmandu into the western mountains takes us to Arughat - Soti Khola(700m altitude) via Dhadingbesi, headquarter of the Dhading district
    • En route we will stop for meal
    • Through the dirt road from Dhadingbesi viewing the greenery hills we reach Arughat then Soti Khola, our trek start point; at bank of Budhi Gandaki river
    • Overnight stay in lodge
    • Meals included: Breakfast Lunch and Dinner

  • Day 3: Soti Khola - Machha Khola

    5-6 hours Tea House 14.1 km
    • Cross the bridge and trek through the beautiful Sal forests, then climb onto a ridge above huge rapids on the Budhi Gandaki. The rocky trail then weaves its way up and down, past two tropical waterfalls on a steep, rocky trail clinging to the side of a cliff
    • . It eventually makes its way down and past a few rice terraces, then up and around to the Gurung village of Labubesi. Climb behind a rocky outcrop, where the valley opens and the Budhi Gandaki meanders among wide gravel bars. Drop to the sandy river bed and walk along rounded stones before climbing over a side ridge
    • Head down again to the river and traverse to Machha Khola village (887m altitude)
    • Overnight stay at Machha Khola
    • Meals included: Breakfast Lunch and Dinner

  • Day 4: Machha Khola - Jagat

    6-7 hours Lodge 22.2 km
    • The narrow trail makes some minor ups and downs and eventually crosses the Tharo Khola, flowing in a rocky ravine, then reaches Khorlabesi
    • After a few more ups & downs there is a small trailside hotspring, from where we reach to Tatopani. From the Hot spring we climb over another ridge, then cross the Budhi Gandaki on a suspension bridge. Climb on a wide, well-crafted staircase, then across a landslide and over a ridge to Dobhan. Crossing a suspension bridge over the Dobhan Khola, the trail climbs on a rugged trail to Duman
    • Ascending up again to the Budhi Gandaki, we reach at Yaru Khola. Crossing a suspension bridge over the Yaru Khola we climb the stone stairs and then drop to the river and again climb more stone stairs to Tharo Bharyang. Cross to the west bank of the budhi gandaki, climb over a ridge, and trek along the river then climb towards the village of Jagat (1345m altitude)
    • Overnight stay at Jagat
    • Meals included: Breakfast Lunch and Dinner

  • Day 5: Jagat - Lokpa

    6-7 hours Lodge 14 km
    • After completing with the ACAP procedure, we climb over a rocky ridge to Salleri, and then descend to Sirdibas
    • The valley widens a bit as the trail continues up to Ghatta Khola. Continue upstream to a long simple hanging bridge. Climb up to Philim, a large gurung village. Philim valley is well known for Japanese made school and police station
    • Walk past Philim to the north across a forest with the views of the narrowing valley and walk up north from Gum Pul we reach Lokpa (2260m altitude)
    • Overnight stay at Lokpa
    • Meals included: Breakfast Lunch and Dinner

  • Day 6: Lokpa - Chumling (lower Tsum)

    4-5 hours Lodge
    • From Lokpa the trail descend half an hour towards Lungwa khola and climbing jick jack difficult trail further two hours north through pines and rhododendrons we reach Gumlung
    • Crossing the Siyar Khola, finally arrive at Chumling (2386m altitude), lower Tsum, from where we can view Shringi Himal. Visit the old Chumling gompa, and the stone streets of the village
    • Overnight stay in Lokpa
    • Meals included: Breakfast Lunch and Dinner

  • Day 7: Chumling - Chhokangparo

    4-5 hours Lodge
    • Cross the suspension bridge onto the opposite bank. Enjoy the great views of Ganesh Himal. Walk past Rainjam to Serpu Khola crossing another bridge we arrive at the Gho Village
    • Another continues 2 hours climb to Tsum takes us to the village of Chhokangparo (3031m altitude). Weather permitting; enjoy the views of Himalchuli at 7893 meters and Ganesh Himal at 7140m
    • Walking across the rocky hillside routes, youâ€™ll finally reach the Lho
    • Overnight stay in Chokkangparo
    • Meals included: Breakfast Lunch and Dinner

  • Day 8: Chokkangparo - Nile

    5-6 hours Lodge
    • We trek through the villages of Ngakyu. Leru, Lama Gaun today
    • We will visit a cave and pay homage. It is believed that saint Milarepa had meditated here in the cave; (admission fee to visit the cave was NPR 300/U$3) the cave is also known as Piren Phu meaning â€œPigeon cave"
    • We continue passing Burji, Phurbe and Pangdun villages, there is magnificent big Buddhist Stupas is sighted on the way to Chhule and Nile (3347m altitude)
    • Overnight stay in Nile
    • Meals included: Breakfast Lunch and Dinner

  • Day 9: Mu Gompa(3700m) & Dhephyudonma Gompa (4060m) sightseeing

    5-6 hours Lodge
    • We are now almost close to the Tibetan border. Walk to the west bank of the valley through the Tibetan landscapes
    • Climb up to the Mu Gompa. We also visit the monastery. Then after, we make a visit to the Dhephu Doma Gompa. Today we make a sightseeing of the whole Mu Gompa Region. Mu Gompa is the largest monastery in the region
    • The Gompa lies at the highest and most remote part of the Tsum Valley. After exploring the Mu Gompa area overnight stay in Nile
    • Meals included: Breakfast Lunch and Dinner

  • Day 10: Nile - Chhekamparo

    4-5 hours Lodge
    • Today it is a easy walk to Chhekamparo (3031m altitude) from Nile. We trek passing through Lar Village visit the Rachen Gompa, which is also well known as Nunnery Gompa too, cross over a suspension bridge and trek to Chhekamparo via same trail back
    • Overnight stay in Chhekamparo
    • Meals included: Breakfast Lunch and Dinner

  • Day 11: Chhekamparo - Gumba Lungdang

    6-7 hours Lodge
    • The trail drops down from Chhekamparo to Gho and then cross Dumje Bridge. We can visit a Amchi, a Tibetan Traditional clinic and there
    • There is a nun; teaches some 10-21 young nun and runs small Amchi. She welcomes the visitor by Tibetan tea, we walk up about 2-3 hours that takes us to Gumba Lungdang (3200m altitude), there basic shelter and meals is provided by monastery
    • Wilderness Excursion will provide tent and extra meal for our clientâ€™s visitng Gumba Lungdang
    • Meals included: Breakfast Lunch and Dinner

  • Day 12: Gumba Lungdang - Ganesh Himal Base Camp

    4-5 hours Lodge
    • Your guide will be required for this trip since the track is poorly marked. Circle from the gompa past the nunsâ€™ housing, drop on dusty or muddy zigzags to regain the lower track and continue up valley on a rocky indistinct track through the forest
    • Cross the Laudang Khola to the west bank on a rickety wooden bridge and climb steeply through pristine pines and rhododendrons on a ridge
    • There is a hut in a kharka about halfway up, with the track continuing behind it, then up a birch-lined dry creek bed and eventually you emerge into grassy flats behind the lateral moraine of the Toro Gompa glacier. Continue climbing past seasonal yak huts and you will find a track on the moraine wall that gives superb views of the Cirque of mountains. The camp is somewhere about here. It takes about 4hrs to reach the Ganesh Himal Base Camp (5000m)
    • The map shows another base camp on the east side of the glacier, but there appears to be no obvious track between them, so return to Gumba Lungdang in time for the evening puja by retracing your steps. Altitude can make this day difficult for some, but the intact forest wilderness and views make it an outstanding trip
    • Overnight stay at Monastery
    • Meals included: Breakfast Lunch and Dinner

  • Day 13: Ganesh Himal Base Camp - Domje -Lokpa

    9-10 hours Lodge
    • After the wonderful stay at Ganesh Himal Base Camp, we hike bake to Domje. The hike goes towards Torogumba Glacier and to Langdang Khola. We pass the beautiful forest, terrace lands and the great scenario with the narrow steep path.
    • The trail descends towards to Landang Khola and we cross this with a swing bridge and passes along the forest. We reach Ripchet after crossing another swing bridge from where we can take the picture as our wish. We can pass the fertile land of barley and buckwheat on the trail. The trek descends with the loose steep path and cross the river again and ascends towards Lokpa (2240m altitude) via pristine tempered forest
    • Overnight stay in Lokpa
    • Meals included: Breakfast Lunch and Dinner

  • Day 14: Lokpa - Jagat

    5-6 hours Lodge
    • Today, after breakfast at Lokpa, we walk down to Jagat. The trail goes down to Ghumpul through the pine and rhododendron forest and join to the main Manaslu trail again
    • The trail passes the lush forests full of flora & fauna including Rhododendron bushes
    • We continue walk passin through Eklai Bhatti, Philim, crossing Buddhi Gandaki before reaching Sirdibas and finally to Jagat (1340m altitude)
    • Overnight stay in Jagat
    • Meals included: Breakfast Lunch and Dinner

  • Day 15: Lokpda - Lapubesi

    5-6 hours Lodge
    • From Jagat, the trail goes down to Bagar after walking some 15 minutes then cross the suspension bridge and walk through cantilever bridge that was built after the Nepal earthquake that hit country so badly and cause famous Manaslu trekking trail damage
    • Contune the jouney via Dovan, Tatopani (hot spring), Khorlabesi and finally stay overnight in Lapubesi (1008m altitude)
    • Overnight stay in Jagat
    • Meals included: Breakfast Lunch and Dinner

  • Day 16: Lapubesi - Arughat

    6-7 hours Lodge
    • Today, it is last day of our trekking in Tsum Valley. We walk to Arughat (941m) from Lapu Bes
    • Overnight stay in Arughat
    • Meals included: Breakfast Lunch and Dinner

  • Day 17: Arughat - Kathmandu

    7-8 hours Lodge 125.1 km
    • We drive to Kathmandu by local bus. There is no tourist bus available at Arughat. Wilderness Excursion can arrange 4WD private jeep that is more comfortable and safer drive if you would not like local bus that is overloaded sometimes, playing non-stop music
    • A farewell dinner is organized by Wilderness Excursion in one of the authentic Nepalese restaurant
    • Overnight stay in Kathmandu
    • Meals included: Breakfast Lunch and Dinner

  • Day 18: Departure from Kathmandu

    • This is the final day where the clients are dropped off from the hotel to the airport for departure
