Lower Mansalu Trek

About The Place

Beautifully settled villages, warm and welcoming locals and their unique culture and traditions, traditional art and craftsmanship and wonderful views of snowcapped mountains are the highlight for the tourist in Lower Manaslu Trek which gives full satisfaction of their journey for lifetime experience.

Itinerary

  • Day 1 : Arrival at Kathmandu (1,400m)

    Lodge
    • Arrival at Kathmandu (Tribhuvan International Airport)
    • Short brief about trek to Clients
    • Dinner & Overnight Stay at Lodge

  • Day 2 : Kathmandu to Gorkha

    3-4 hours Lodge 142.9 km
    • Drive from Kathmandu to Gorkha Bazar (900m)
    • Explore around old Palace, and town
    • Dinner & overnight stay at lodge

  • Day 3: Gorkha – Sirandanda

    2-3 hours Home Stay 53.4 km
    • We drive to Sirandanda (1900m) by local bus or jeep
    • It takes 6-7 hours to Sirandanda, a curvaceous drive through mountain roads overlooking Daraudi River is thrilling and scenic
    • Dinner & overnight stay in home stay.

  • Day 4: Sirandanda – Barpak Village

    1-2 hours Home Stay
    • Today we trek to Barpak Village (1920m) which is known as Village of army men and also recognized as village of the late Capt. Gaje Ghale who was honored with Victoria Cross Medal of Great Britain. The village has 24 hours electricity, internet and well organized home stay along with good hotels facility and bus access from Kathmandu during dry season
    • The unique sloppy shaped with clustered stone roof houses, great Himalayan view are the major attractions of the Barpak village
    • Dinner & overnight stay at homestay

  • Day 5: Barpak Village – Laprak Village

    5-6 hours Community Lodge 5.7km
    • Our trek continues to huge Laprak Village (2100m). On the way to Laprak we come across Basukang (a junction that leads to Rupina la, (a famous Manaslu alternative trekking trail) Main notable feature of Laprak is wooden roofed houses, Gurung culture and awesome landscape
    • Dinner & overnight stay in community lodge

  • Day 6: Laprak Village – Namabai Kharka

    4-5 hours Home Stay
    • A moderate 4-5 hours walk from Laprak takes us to Nambai Kharka (2700m, pasture or cowshed).
    • It is a open land pasture of Laprak people. There are some temporary houses and no toilet facility. It is believed that slaughtering animals bring illness to trekkers so we our service on this days is only vegetation
    • Dinner & overnight stay in home stay

  • Day 7: Namabai Kharkha - Dharche Danda(3220m) - Tallo Thotneri (1,658m)

    6-7 hours Guest House
    • Hike to Dharche Danda (3220m), the highest pint in this trip which offers a breath taking 180 degree panoramic view of more than 20 Himalayan peaks. According to locals, the hill holds religious significance. They offer prayers to the hill and avoid natural calamity. Stay overnight in same place
    • . It is believed that if the tourist offers prayers to the hill they will be blessed and ward off misfortune. Some of the prominent peaks that can be seen are: Annapurna, Annapurna I, Annapurna II, Barashikhar, Gangapurna, Machhapuchhre, Langtang, Himal Chuli, Buddha Himal, Sringi Himal, Ganesh I, Ganesh II, Ganesh III, among many others
    • Locals share their cultural dances and delicacies
    • Dinner & overnight stay in homestay

  • Day 8: Tallo Thotneri – Ghyampesal(1029m)

    6-7 hours Home Stay
    • We trek to Ghyampesal via adventurous Sita Cave
    • After touring the cave we walk to Ghyampesal, our final destination of our trek
    • Dinner and stay overnight in home stay

  • Day 9: Ghyampesal – Gorkha Bazaar - Kathmandu

    8-9 hours Hotel 11.3km
    • We take 2.5 hours drive from Ghyamsepal to Gorkha Bazaar
    • And then about 6 hours of drive from Gorkha Bazaar to Kathmandu
    • The guide wil drop you off to the hotel in Kathmandu. Get fresh and relax
